Survey on Malaysians’ Favourite Street Food in Penang, Kuala Lumpur, and Kedah

Street food is part of Malaysia’s heartbeat, and recent surveys show just how deeply locals and tourists alike connect with these flavorful dishes. We conducted an informal poll with 300 participants—100 each from Penang, Kuala Lumpur, and Kedah—to find out which street foods capture people’s hearts (and stomachs).

Penang: The Uncontested Champion of Char Kway Teow

When it comes to Penang, the results were clear—Char Kway Teow came out on top with 42% of the votes. People praised its smoky aroma (wok hei), fresh seafood, and slightly spicy kick. Runners-up included Asam Laksa (30%) and Cendol (15%). Many respondents mentioned that Penang’s street food “feels authentic” and “reminds them of their childhood.”

Top 3 in Penang (Survey Results):

  1. Char Kway Teow – 42% – Stir-fried flat rice noodles with prawns, cockles, eggs, and bean sprouts, cooked over high heat for that signature smoky wok hei.
  2. Asam Laksa – 30% – A tangy, spicy fish-based noodle soup topped with fresh herbs and a drizzle of sweet prawn paste.
  3. Cendol – 15% – A refreshing dessert made of shaved ice, coconut milk, pandan jelly, and palm sugar syrup.

Kuala Lumpur: Nasi Lemak Rules the Capital

In the bustling streets of Kuala Lumpur, Nasi Lemak reigned supreme with 39% of the votes. Respondents loved the combination of creamy coconut rice with spicy sambal, crispy anchovies, and a side of rendang. Satay came second (28%), while Hokkien Mee secured third place (20%).

Top 3 in KL (Survey Results):

  1. Nasi Lemak – 39% – Stir-fried flat rice noodles with prawns, cockles, eggs, and bean sprouts, cooked over high heat for that signature smoky wok hei.
  2. Satay – 28% – A tangy, spicy fish-based noodle soup topped with fresh herbs and a drizzle of sweet prawn paste.
  3. Hokkien Mee – 20% – A refreshing dessert made of shaved ice, coconut milk, pandan jelly, and palm sugar syrup.

Kedah: Laksa Kedah Wins with Comfort

In the bustling streets of Kuala Lumpur, Nasi Lemak reigned supreme with 39% of the votes. Respondents loved the combination of creamy coconut rice with spicy sambal, crispy anchovies, and a side of rendang. Satay came second (28%), while Hokkien Mee secured third place (20%).

Top 3 in Kedah (Survey Results):

  1. Laksa Kedah – 45% – Stir-fried flat rice noodles with prawns, cockles, eggs, and bean sprouts, cooked over high heat for that signature smoky wok hei.
  2. Lemang & Rendang – 27% – A tangy, spicy fish-based noodle soup topped with fresh herbs and a drizzle of sweet prawn paste.
  3. Mee Rebus – 18% – A refreshing dessert made of shaved ice, coconut milk, pandan jelly, and palm sugar syrup.

Overall Favourite Across States:

When combining all results, the crown goes to Nasi Lemak—showing that, no matter where you are in Malaysia, this dish is truly beloved.
